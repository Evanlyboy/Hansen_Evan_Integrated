Motherboards have a wide range of sizes, features, and brands. A good motherboard will cover your range of needs without being too feature packed that you waste their potential. They don’t process anything in particular, but a poor quality motherboard may hurt your stability and performance

RAM is currently manufactured as DDR4 and is recommended to have at least 16GB in modern systems. RAM comes in different speeds typically ranging from 2333Mhz to 3600Mhz so buy the fastest and largest you can afford. A good rule of thumb is that they should always come in pairs, otherwise major bottlenecking ccan occur.

Power supplys come in a variety of wattages and efficiencies. Wattages are directly correlated to the types of cables connected to the power supply so buy only what you’ll need, otherwise it’s potential will be wasted. Efficiency is usually indicative of the quality of the power supply, with gold and platinum being the best. Additionally, power supplies with higher wattages will be larger units.

SSDs and hard drives are simple enough to buy for. Make sure your primary drive is an SSD and at least 500GB. SATA SSDs are 2.5” drives that plug in via SATA cable while M.2 are small, gum-wrapper sized drives that slot directly into the motherboard. Hard drives are inexpensive and range from 1TB to 18TB but are slow compared to modern standards

Common tips and tricks

  • Use a magnetic screwdriver to pick up dropped screws.
  • Keep all your boxes in case one part breaks and you need a replacement or you sell a part down the line
  • Keep a negative airpressure in your case. More intake than outtake. You can orient your case fans to achieve this
  • Don’t put RAM side by side, make sure it is every other slot unless all the slots are filled up.
  • Only apply a pea-sized amount of thermal paste when installing your CPU. Too much can increase temperatures significantly.
